CS376: Human Computer Interaction

In my Human-Computer Interaction (CS376) class at Williams College, we delved into the key elements of design from multiple perspectives, including interactivity, effectiveness, responsibility, and accessibility. Throughout the course, we adopted a user-centered mindset, which guided our approach to understanding the intricacies of design. The class involved hands-on activities and projects that allowed us to deeply analyze these elements and interpret them within real-world contexts. One of the core aspects of the class was engaging in four design sprints: Design for Others, Design for Interaction, Design for Effectiveness, and Design for Responsibility. These sprints provided a structured framework through which we could explore design principles and apply them to our projects.

The course also helped me hone several project development skills, including planning, time and resource management, collaboration, and iterative development. We worked in teams, which fostered a collaborative environment and helped us understand how to coordinate efforts efficiently. A unique aspect of the course was the roleplaying activity, where we simulated the experience of being a diverse group of individuals in a product development team, aiming to deliver a high-quality product. This activity not only emphasized the importance of communication but also showcased the challenges of balancing differing perspectives and needs during the design process.

Beyond the theoretical knowledge and technical skills, the class encouraged real-world application. We were tasked with exploring our campus environment, identifying accessible and inaccessible features, and testing our designs with students and faculty members. This allowed us to see how our work could directly impact the user experience and make a tangible difference. Additionally, we documented our design processes through detailed Medium posts, which further developed our documentation and organizational skills. Overall, CS376 provided a comprehensive understanding of HCI principles and prepared us for the practical challenges designers and developers face in real-life projects.
