Ephs Smartwatching for Health

In my needfinding assignment, I explored how smartwatches are transforming personal health technology by interviewing three individuals at Williams College. The focus was on their experiences, motivations, and challenges in using these devices. Smartwatches, valued for tracking vital health metrics like heart rate and sleep, offer unique insights into users' daily routines and health awareness. Each participant shared how these tools have enhanced their lives, from simplifying fitness tracking to predicting illness through heart rate variability.

The interviews also revealed significant challenges. While users appreciated features like gentle alarms and health reminders, inaccuracies emerged in unexpected scenarios, such as undercounting steps while pushing a stroller or misinterpreting activities as sleep. Additionally, the high cost of smartwatches posed a barrier, making some question the balance between their benefits and expenses. These limitations highlight opportunities for innovation in accuracy and affordability.

This assignment was a fascinating and valuable experience for me, as it deepened my understanding of user needs and their critical role in designing or improving products. I also learned about empathy maps as a powerful tool for organizing user insights and identifying areas for innovation. Engaging with users helped me appreciate the importance of empathy in designing impactful, user-centered solutions that effectively balance usability, cost, and functionality.
